Title: The Last Dragonlord
(Buy it now, click the link.)
Author: Joanne Bertin
Rating: Mature*
Review: ……………
5 out of 5 Aliens Reccomend it.
Synopsis: A trio of weredragons are brought in to break up a political struggle in the country of Cassna. Meanwhile a revitilization of the political group set to destroy the Dragonlords is manipulating things behind the scenes.
General:

First of all, its rare in the highly franchised world of Science-Fiction and Fantasy to find a good first book in a new series of such a high caliber as this one. Even though the books sometimes delve into the romantic whiles, its very discreet, and it does well to add to this deeply intriquing story. Full of political intrigue, maybe not as all-pervasive as Herbert's Dune, it still is very well done. The brilliant imagery of new writer Joanne Bertin describes a world large and full of many unique cultures. Her characters, especially that of Linden Rathan and Bard Otter Heronson feel as if we have grown up with them, not that we are reading about them for the first time. She has a great potential for writing both sequels and prequels, further developing her unique world.
Magic:

Magic is one of the tools of the fantasy trade. Overused as it is, most series' follow the similar style of magic. Joanne Bertin proves her abilities by bringing into play her own style of magic. Her magic manifests itself in many forms, the 'evil' blood magic, the 'wild magic' which inadvertently created the Dragonlords who serve Humanity, and most likely other forms of magic that are yet to be seen.
Mature:

The book makes free use of a small collection of curse words. Her romantic usage is always referential, never dirty with details.
Conclusion:

This book is one that won't leave you for a while, remiscent of Pern, and it won't be surprising to hear about groups of people on the Internet role-playing in Joanne Bertin's incredible world because it is one that you will want to revisit.
