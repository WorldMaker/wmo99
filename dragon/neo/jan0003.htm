Part 4

 

                                               

Grinning from pointy ear to pointy ear, the Empress of Smoke laughed,

 

‘Well, that’s some work well done, Gyrfalcon! Be off, now, and sure those Panther Wardens outside are killed! Be off, Cardinal Gyrfalcon!’

 

My chest swelling with pride, I marched out, my feathers fluttering as  I stepped down the hall.

 

Suddenly, I noticed that all the other birds were flying as I passed them, so, trying hard to seem as the Captain I was, I flapped my magnificent wings and was pleasantly surprised to find my sleek feathered body gliding through the air down the hall, but something was wrong. I was sagging along, halting every now and then. Looking at how the other birds flew, noticed all the others had their legs tucked under their body as they sailed through the air. I tucked my taloned feet under my torso and was amazed at how I flew.

 

Testing my new ability, I moved my wings even faster up and down, flapping the feathered engines. Then, when I had enough momentum going, I bent my wings so they were close to my body, but still spread, so I was gliding along with, what it seemed like, no wings. I moved my wings occasionally to build more speed, but that was all.

 

I was now traveling at a breakneck rapidity, and birds and bucks alike moved out of my way as I sped down the mezzanine. Many noticed that I was a gyrfalcon, which made me the Cardinal Minion. Any bird of prey that wore the golden collar I wore had to be the Captain, for no pigeon could take the role.

 

I was now doing cartwheels in the air, flying upside-down and doing loop-the-loops, and pretty much having a wonderful time. The black markings around my face that made up a mask made me feel invisible as I hurtled along. Then I realized that I hadn’t seen Elf since I woke up. Rocketing into a chamber on my right, I moved my wings forward and backed air, stopping myself as I fluttered to the ground. I viewed a bird standing on a perch, sleeping. It wore the silver collar of a minion, not gold like mine. 

 

‘You!’ I barked

 

The small bird’s eyes snapped open and stared at me for a moment. Then, realizing who I was, sprang from his perch, landing on the ground, a mere apple next to my size.

 

‘Cardinal! I’m sorry! I didn’t know it was you!’

 

‘What’s your name?’ I screeched.

 

The small gray bird with blackish spots bobbed its head up and down.

 

‘Ch-chickadee, sir... er, marm!’

 

I nodded and spoke calmly, blinking my eyes that were two large amber pools.

 

‘Good… good… The Empress of Smoke directed me to come to you.’

 

‘Me, marm?’

 

‘Yes, you! Now, tell me this, fellow minion, where is the Pegastag that arrived last night?’

 

            The chickadee swallowed hard and blinked its pinpoint eyes.

 

            ‘In the stable downstairs, marm. Shall I direct you, marm?’

 

            ‘No.’ and again I flapped my wings hard and tucked them close to my body, flying accelerated down the hall and down the puncheon stairs, gliding over a stag that was coming up.

 

            I found myself slowing my rythym to a steady beat of wings as I searched the stables for Elf.

 

            ‘Ho! You there, what’s your business down here?’

 

            I cocked a bright eye on the jackdaw that spoke, seeing him quail under my glare.

 

            ‘C-cardinal, I didn’t realize…’

 

            ‘Enough!’ I squawked ‘Just show me to he stable of the Pegastag that was brought here last nightfall!’

 

            The Jackdaw sidled in front of my dignified pace, showing me to a large, airy stall with fresh cedar in it. There stood Elf, munching something that smelled like honey.

 

            I nodded my release to the jackdaw and entered the stall in a flutter of wings. Elf recognized me as Lily and continued munching her sweet grain.

           

            ‘Hey, girl,’ I crooned. ‘they taking good care of you?’ she nuzzled me and I petted her a bit before leaving her to her comfit. I glided at my breakneck speed out of the stables past an alarmed jackdaw, up the stairs and back to the doors of the Sun Fox.

 

            I burst in on her, sitting on the throne watching the sudden gentle rain that fell from the skies. I mentally knew it was not a good time for the Panther Wardens that stalked outside the gates, which made me smile a gyrfalcon smile.

 

            ‘Majesty of Fire,’ I said breathlessly, ‘thank you for making me the bird I am! I have had such fun!’

 

            She turned to me, a smile playing about her lips and she remarked amusingly,

 

‘You should have had some fun for being gone three hours!’

 

            My beak dropped open in disbelief.

 

            ‘Three hours, Vixen of Realms? Surely not!’

 

            ‘Yes,’ she chuckled, ‘it’s hard to keep track of time when you are flying and winging about, am I right?’

 

            I could only nod.

 

            This time when she spoke, her voice was serious and she gave me an order.

 

            ‘Gyrfalcon, please take all the fiercest birds, like the shrikes, for instance, and have them posted on the north, south, east, and west walls of Malkarizz. The Panthers are planning an attack, says certain information.’

 

            I raised my eyebrow questioningly, but kept silent.

 

            ‘Dismissed! Now go do my bidding!’

 

            Bowing low, I turned and rocketed out of the chamber. I breezed through the corridors of My Lady’s castle, screeching,

 

            ‘All shrikes, butcherbirds, ravens, jackdaws, and other fierce birds out to the north wall!’ I heard the heavy flutter of wings behind me as I blazed down the halls.

 

            Once on the north wall, I turned to look at what assortment I had. Two falcons, counting myself, many crows, jackdaws, many shrikes and butcherbirds, harriers, a kestrel, one hawk, one raven, and a scattering of gulls.

 

            ‘All right, fellow minions,’ I screeched above the crowd, ‘I want five gulls up here next to me.’ A flutter of wings announced their flight to my side. ‘The other five to the south fall, alert to any panthers. If they try to attack, make a feathered fury to discourage their entrance. I want ten shrikes on the west wall, ten on the east wall. The other falcon on the south wall. Eight butcherbirds up here, seven on the south wall. Three jackdaws on the east, four crows on the west. The hawk and the kestrel, come up here, and you three harriers, on the south… no, the east, and the raven up here as well. There, to your posts!’

 

There was much  cawing and straining of wings as my peers left the group and went to where I planted them.

 

A cry was heard as a gull went down with a panther claw in his throat, and another as the panther was lifted into the air and dropped by the hawk, the kestrel, and I, screaming to the ground.

 

We gave a fair account of ourselves and only three were killed, not counting the gull. The panthers soon ran for cover as the shrikes zoomed from the walls and dive bombed the felines. I followed suit and went at my expeditious speed, amazing the other birds as I passed them up with careless ease. My fearsome curved beak ripped a panther between the eyes as I dropped from the sky like a stone. I then lifted again before striking the ground with the bloodlust hot in my eyes. I struck down panthers left and right until there was no more of them left on the ground to strike.

 

With a triumphant screech, I called my troops back into Malkarizz, shaking talons and patting backs with my wing. Each of my fighters had a happy, satisfied look in their eye and I released them to the kitchens to have a rest and a drink of water.

 

I glided, unburdened with war, back to The Queen of Light’s chamber door, knocking with my talon.

 

‘Come in,’ came her merry voice.

 

I entered, flapping in with newfound certainty.

 

‘Oh, it’s you, Gyrfalcon,’ she beamed, ‘I saw you and your comrades fight. You did beautifully. You are natural born to the air, as if you were born in the sky. You also have quite the fighting talent.’

 

I raised a talon and she nodded permission.

 

‘Majesty of Fire, don’t you have any ospreys among your army?’

 

The Sun Fox nodded.

 

‘Yes, weren’t they out there with you?’

 

I shook my great sleek feathered head.

 

‘No, O Queen of Light, there was not one osprey among my fighters in that battle.’

 

She nodded her head knowingly.

 

‘My ospreys are determined and stubborn. They probably are sour to you, for you are much faster, stronger, and bigger than they. You took the place of an Osprey Cardinal, they are most likely surly to that fact. If you would like to speak with them, they usually roost in the cellars behind the ales.’

 

I bowed and exited the room.

 

Swooping swiftly into a small room, I turned stiffly to a group of bluebirds that perched together, speaking about the battle. I caught snatches of their conversation.

 

‘They say that new Gyrfalcon is quite the Chief.’

‘Aye, she won that battle without lifting a talon.’

‘She’s very intelligent. Great strategies for the fight.’

 

I flew to an empty perch in the group and gripped it with my sharp talons.

 

‘Excuse me,  my peers, would you please direct me close to the concoction chamber?’

 

One pointed down the hall with a wing breathlessly.

 

As I flew briskly out of the room, I heard two say,

 

‘Gracious, look at the talon marks in that perch!’

‘My, she do talk lovely!’
